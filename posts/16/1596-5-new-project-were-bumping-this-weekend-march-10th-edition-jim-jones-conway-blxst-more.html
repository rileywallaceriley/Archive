5 New Projects We're Bumping This Weekend, March 10th Edition: Jim Jones, Conway, BLXST & More

Fri, — aboveaveragehiphop.com

Jim Jones & HitMaka - Back In My Prime

Harlem-based rapper Jim Jones has teamed up with producer HitMaka to release their latest EP, Back In My Prime--an eight-track project that showcases Jones' ability to adapt to the ever-changing sounds in hip-hop culture.

Released through EMPIRE, the EP highlights Jones' lyrical prowess and unique style, from the introspective "Lose Lose" to the upbeat and energetic "Make No Issues of It." Fans can expect to hear the rapper's signature ad-libs and hard-hitting beats combined with HitMaka's polished production style.

Zaytoven - Street Credentials

Grammy award-winning producer Zaytoven drops his highly anticipated album Street Credentials via Raven Music/MNRK Urban.

The album features an impressive lineup of artists, including G-Herbo, Key Glock, OJ Da Juiceman, Doe Boy, Bobby Fishscales, and Shy Glizzy, among others. "I wanted to make an album that reflects the streets and the people who live there," Zaytoven explains. "I've worked with some of the biggest names in the game, but this album is about going back to my roots and bringing that real, authentic sound to the forefront."

BLXST - Just For Clarity 2

After a breakthrough year that included two Grammy nominations and recognition on prominent "Best of 2022" lists, Blxst returns with the next chapter to his 2021 EP, Just For Clarity 2.

The four-shot EPfeatures his hit single "Keep Calling" featuring Larry June as well as collaborations with names like Roddy Ricch (on the standout single "Passionate") and Mustard and Terrace Martin (on the romantic track "Ghetto Cinderella").

Mach-hommy & Tha God Fahim - Notorious Dump Legends: Volume 2

Just a day after a three-part interview was released on The FADER, the always elusive Mach-Hommy dropped his first new record of the year, Notorious Dump Legends: Volume 2.

A collaboration with Tha God Fahim. a follow-up to the original released in 2018 (widely considered an early standout of the duo's collaborations).
