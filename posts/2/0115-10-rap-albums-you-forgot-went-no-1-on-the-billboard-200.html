10 Rap Albums You Forgot Went No. 1 On The Billboard 200

2020-04-19 — editorials

Though many albums achieve success, not all records manage to top the illustrious Billboard 200, a chart that ranks albums of all genres.

Historically, it’s taken some of the game’s elite a few cracks at it to capture the top spot.Here at HipHopDX, we’ve compiled a list of 10 albums that once held the No. 1 position — some of which you may have all but forgotten.Scarface - The UntouchableWeeks On The Top: March 29, 1997https://open.spotify.com/album/0yEbGVnKvWj4B85rzExJmF?si=CJE5Pa82Syi8mgDorx-auAThe follow-up to The Diary (his first platinum solo LP), Scarface’s fourth album included the hit single “Smile” featuring the late Tupac Shakur, which stands as his most successful, earning gold certification and peaking at No. 12 on the Hot 100.2Pac - Loyal To The GameWeeks On The Top: January 1, 2005https://open.spotify.com/album/33lk2GnP8cJzl7cd1OJrXn?si=zxhfHgSmQ825dKYDyItGFwAfter writing a letter to Afeni Shakur, Eminem was permitted to produce the entirety of this project, which was comprised of remixes made from outtakes and non-album cuts off Pac’s first three albums.JAY-Z - Kingdom Come Weeks On The Top: December 9, 2006https://open.spotify.com/album/3A29Ss77LjRZ3sKuauWieY?si=drWoAQ75SgKAK7kEpTXdwwThough it’s often regarded as one of Hov’s lowest-ranked albums, his post-retirement “comeback” earned itself two platinum plaques — amid a pile of lukewarm reviews.Ludacris - Chicken-N-BeerWeeks On The Top: October 25, 2003https://open.spotify.com/album/3hJHXxX04PNuGFIxdEHGzg?si=O6X8kYtOT5CdTj-lmFjdhgThis album spawned four singles, two of which, “Stand Up” and “Splash Waterfalls,” landed him in the Billboard Hot 100’s top 10 for the first time in his career.Master P - MP Da Last DonWeeks On The Top: June 20-27, 1998https://open.spotify.com/album/0BVvg6EVfwto3LmLbhEAnM?si=ToIYuM_NQFO_S9rcG_DxGQYounger listeners may not understand the grandeur, but Master P was red-hot in 1998 when he announced that this would be his final album.

Of course, it wasn’t — Only God Can Judge Me dropped the following year.

MP da Last Don remains his best selling release.B.o.B - B.o.B Presents: The Adventures Of Bobby RayWeeks On The Top: May 15, 2010https://open.spotify.com/album/7apLPYT8szV1IqTxyVSy5P?si=irRTPjpaSh2aJkGgYd715QLupe Fiasco’s lazy version of "Nothin' on You" being scrapped and given to B.o.B was a blessing that (along with the Eminem-featured “Airplanes”) propelled this charming, well-aged body of music to commercial success.

It also introduced Bruno Mars to the world.A Tribe Called Quest - Beats, Rhymes and LifeWeeks On The Top: August 17, 1996https://open.spotify.com/album/0EguP4tsJurU5I8ocCxdyb?si=NdVmfQJZQreVO2nUGmuJjQIn hindsight, many regard this as one of their weakest albums.

However, this Grammy-nominated gem introduced the world to Consequence and the late-great J. Dilla — who, along with The Ummah, gave this album a slightly darker edge than its three predecessors.2 Chainz - Based On A T.R.U. StoryWeeks On The Top: September 1, 2012https://open.spotify.com/album/5fL5QvYTODefwIrQVAHa0f?si=iJ-4oW4VRNaAxyV5Fhno8QFollowing massive appearances on some of the biggest singles of the day — most notably Kanye West’s “Mercy” (along with Big Sean) — 2 Chainz dropped his official debut, led by the Drake-assisted “No Lie.”Wale - The Album About NothingWeeks On The Top: April 18, 2015https://open.spotify.com/album/6aCr3UaB8lM9g6TtFB3qwd?si=lvO7ymYhRyKl8HoIqufJRwThe apex of his Seinfeld-themed mixtape series, this album helped the DC-native score his second number No. 1 album following the success of The Gifted.

Sidebar: the album is aging beautifully.Tha Dogg Pound - Dogg FoodWeeks On The Top: November 18, 1995https://open.spotify.com/album/7l1KUvadF1rcUXRhviJXXD?si=y9T8GMbgS1SVnjEUlQhGpAConsidered to be the last hurrah of both the often imitated (never duplicated) G-Funk era and Death Row’s original run, the first independent album to top Billboard put the spotlight on Daz Dillinger as a producer – and further fanned the flames of the East Coast vs.

West Coast beef with the video for the single ”New York, New York.”
