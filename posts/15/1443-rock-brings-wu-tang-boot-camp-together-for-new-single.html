Rock Brings Wu-Tang & Boot Camp Together For New Single

Wed, — aboveaveragehiphop.com

A little over two years ago the Hip Hop world was rocked by the tragic and sudden loss of Rapper Sean Price, a member of the Boot Camp Clik, and one-half of the legendary duo Heltah Skeltah — alongside Jahmal Bush, better known as Rock.

Finally moving forward (professionally), Rock is making is first post-P foray, Rockness A.P. (After Price), which is--in essence--his first solo-project. In conversation with AAHH back in 2015, Rock noted that he'd been working on his solo for a while. "Situations change, the sounds change, my ears change, and the message I may be trying to give the people may change," he said. "The album is in the process.

Life happens though, and right now.

I just lost the Yin to my Yang … My brother just died.

So, there are changes that need to happen on my album now," he added.

He also shared some kind words and memories about his one-time partner in rhyme. "As unique as we all are as human beings, I mean, you may find somebody else at some point that reminds you of me, but you won’t find anyone that reminds you of him," he said. "He was one of one – and he chose me ." Now, two years following that conversation, we're getting a first taste of what the project has to offer, "Camp-Wu." As the name suggests, it's a collaborative track with members of both Wu-Tang Clan (Inspectah Deck and Method Man) and Boot Camp (Tek and Steele).

The project itself drops on the 22nd and is available through Digital Deja Vu Records.
